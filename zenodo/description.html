In the field of Earth System Science, the comprehensive understanding of complex spatio-temporal environmental processes and the prediction of future trends rely heavily on careful data collection, analysis, and interpretation. Time series data, a critical output of these efforts, play a central role in revealing patterns, identifying changes, and guiding decision making in various environmental domains. Despite the availability of dedicated time series databases to support the development of time series management systems, the effective management of associated metadata, sources, and key factors affecting usability and interpretability remains an ongoing challenge.

The Sensor Management System (SMS), a joint initiative of the Helmholtz Centres UFZ, GFZ, FZJ, and KIT within the DataHub initiative, closes this gap by supporting scientists and technicians in the management of sensors, measurement setups, and campaigns. SMS features a modern and user-friendly interface coupled with a robust JSON:API and the OGC SensorML standard for data exchange. It includes an OAuth-based authentication system using the Helmholtz AAI, complemented by a sophisticated rights management structure. Sensor attributes within SMS adhere to a unified metadata catalogue that includes mandatory, optional, and user-specific attributes. The SMS provides traceability and provenance tracking. Interfaces to handle systems such as B2INST allow persistent identifiers to be generated.

Presently operational at GFZ (https://sensors.gfz-potsdam.de/), UFZ (https://web.app.ufz.de/sms/), and KIT (https://sms.atmohub.kit.edu/), the Sensor Management System provides a decentralized solution. For those choosing self-hosting, comprehensive documentation is available (https://codebase.helmholtz.cloud/hub-terra/sms/orchestration/-/blob/main/README.md). Users seeking guidance on system navigation and features can refer to the WIKI (https://codebase.helmholtz.cloud/hub-terra/sms/service-desk/-/wikis/home) for a user-centric guide and valuable insights into seamless utilization.
